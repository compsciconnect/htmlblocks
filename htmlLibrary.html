All of the HTML code that we write has to go inside an html "tag". Many tags come in pairs <[stuff]> </[stuff]>, the / indicates that this is a closing tag.

You use the body tag to designate which sets of instructions apply to the "body" of the webpage (what you see when you open the site). <html> <body> instructions for what the webpage should do/look like </body> </html>

When you write a research paper you often have a title and different sections like:

My research paper

Information

About birds

About insects

Each of these is called a “heading”. In HTML there are six classes of headings, heading 1 (h1), heading 2 (h2), heading 3 (h3), heading 4 (h4), heading 5 (h5), and heading 6 (h6). Heading 1 is the biggest (like the heading used for “My research paper”) and heading 6 is the smallest.
